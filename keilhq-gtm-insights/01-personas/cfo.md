# CFO View of KeilHQ

**First reaction:** The tool-stack cost comparison is the most legible part of the pitch. The math is simple: Asana/Linear (~$10–16) + Notion (~$8–16) + Slack (~$7–12) + Otter.ai/Fireflies (~$10–19) + Google Workspace (~$6–12) = roughly $41–75/seat/month. KeilHQ's Teams plan is $50/seat/month. That comparison is transparent and the arithmetic is real — the numbers come from public pricing pages, not from KeilHQ-generated figures.

**However:** the CFO will immediately ask what they can verify independently, and the current answer is: "not much beyond the pricing comparison." There is no third-party validation of cost savings, no audited customer outcome data, and no public ROI case studies. The CFO needs to know this is early-stage software, and that the savings claim is directionally accurate but unaudited.

**Champion trigger:** The CFO becomes a champion if:
- The free 30-day trial means zero financial commitment during evaluation — a real risk-removal.
- The billing model is clear and verifiable: per-seat, monthly, with Dodo Payments handling checkout and a billing portal. No hidden fees confirmed from the billing schema.
- Tool consolidation reduces the number of vendor invoices, renewal dates, and accounts to manage — that operational overhead has real cost, even if harder to quantify.
- Data export is available even on expired/locked accounts — confirms no data hostage risk.

**Blocker trigger:**
- "We have negotiated enterprise rates on Slack and Notion — KeilHQ's list pricing doesn't beat our actual blended rate."
- "I need standard purchase orders and invoicing. What is Dodo Payments?" — Dodo Payments is a smaller payment processor, not Stripe. This may create friction with enterprise procurement teams.
- No SOC 2 means any regulated or mid-market company above ~30 people will face a hard procurement blocker before any spend is approved.

*Note: This is illustrative. Some teams may already have negotiated rates below list price. Time-saving claims (e.g., "2 hours/week less context-switching") are plausible but are not verified outcome data — do not present them as guaranteed.*

**Fastest objection:** "This is early-stage software with no audited outcomes. I need proof it actually works before we move budget here." — The 30-day free trial is the right answer. Start with one team. Measure the impact. Come back to the CFO with actual data from your own environment.

**What proof would satisfy them:** A transparent pricing calculator they can reproduce in a spreadsheet (not a marketing claim), plus a clear answer on: what data do we get back if we cancel, and how long does it take?

**Industry/stage variation:**
- **Bootstrapped/seed company:** Every dollar is visible. The direct subscription comparison is compelling. The 30-day trial and the low per-seat price are strong conversion factors.
- **Venture-backed startup:** Time savings are the secondary argument — a tool that prevents rework has leverage on engineering cost, not just subscription cost. But CFO will want to see this measured in their own environment, not asserted.
- **Mid-market or enterprise:** SOC 2 absence is a hard blocker. Dodo Payments will need procurement vetting. This segment is not currently viable without compliance work.
