# Sales (AE / Founder Doing Sales) View of KeilHQ

**First reaction:** "The product demos well — the AI answering a real question about your actual workspace is a moment that generic chatbots can't match. But I need to know what I can and can't promise before I get in a room with a prospect."

**What actually closes deals — confirmed from code:**

1. **The live AI demo** — typing "what's blocking my team right now?" into the KeilHQ AI and getting a real answer grounded in actual tasks is a concrete differentiator. This needs to work reliably in a prospect's own account, not just in a demo environment.

2. **The cost comparison slide** — using published list prices, the math shows KeilHQ at $50/seat beats a 5-tool stack at $41–75/seat. This is defensible when clearly labeled as "based on published list pricing" and not framed as a guaranteed saving.

3. **30-day free trial, no credit card** — this removes the primary conversion blocker. A champion can get the tool in front of their team without needing budget approval.

4. **Public shareable links for tasks and docs** — clients can check project status without a login. For agency sales, this is a concrete, demoable value that replaces a category of email.

5. **GitHub integration (fully implemented)** — for dev teams, showing issues → tasks and tasks → GitHub issues in a live demo is a real differentiator over Notion and Asana.

**What kills deals — be honest upfront:**

- **No mobile app** — any prospect who asks about mobile access on the call will immediately hit a wall. "We're actively building it" is the honest answer. Don't promise a timeline you don't know.
- **No Linear or Jira integration** — if the prospect's dev team is on Linear, KeilHQ cannot fully replace their stack today. The honest answer: "GitHub is live; Linear is on our roadmap." Do not imply Linear integration exists.
- **No Slack integration** — if they're deeply embedded in Slack, KeilHQ's chat is a full replacement, not an add-on. That's a harder migration conversation than a simple integration.
- **No compliance certifications** — if anyone on their team asks about SOC 2, HIPAA, or GDPR, the honest answer is "not yet." Do not imply certifications are in progress if you don't have a timeline. A lie here will end the relationship.
- **Early-stage product** — there is no verified user base to point to. The product is real and functional. Be honest that you're early and that the prospect would be getting in at a stage where their feedback shapes the product.

**Their ROI:** Shorter sales cycle via trial-led conversion — the product sells itself if the trial experience is good. The task is to get the right person (PM, agency owner, dev lead) into a working trial as fast as possible, not to close on slide decks.

**Fastest objection from prospects:** "We already use ClickUp / Monday and paid for a year upfront." — Counter: "You don't have to migrate everything. Start with one team, one project, the next 30 days. If the AI doesn't answer a question about your actual sprint better than anything you have now, cancel. No cost, no migration."

**Battlecard (short, confirmed facts only):**
- vs. Notion: Notion has no real task dependencies, no meeting transcription, no AI with actual task access. Notion AI reads docs — KeilHQ AI reads your sprint state.
- vs. Asana: Asana has no docs, no chat, no meeting transcription, no workspace-aware AI. KeilHQ's Clarity Engine (objectives + success criteria required per task) is structurally different from Asana's freeform tasks.
- vs. ClickUp: ClickUp has everything listed but the AI is not truly workspace-integrated, no meeting transcription with speaker diarization, and no equivalent of the Clarity Engine's enforced definition of done.
- vs. Linear: Linear is engineering-native. KeilHQ adds docs, chat, meeting transcription, and a workspace-aware AI on top of comparable task management. But the Linear integration is not yet live — acknowledge this directly.
