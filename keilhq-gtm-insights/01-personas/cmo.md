# CMO View of KeilHQ

**First reaction:** "There's a real differentiated claim here — the Clarity Engine is genuinely unique in the category. No competitor requires objectives and success criteria at task creation. The AI with real workspace data is a real differentiator over Notion AI or generic chatbots. But the marketing layer on top of this is overstating things we can't prove, and that creates risk."

**Specific problems the CMO will spot:**
- "7,000+ teams globally" — this number appears only in marketing copy and is unverifiable from the codebase or any external source. This is the kind of claim that destroys credibility if a journalist, investor, or competitor asks for proof. It should not appear in any external communication until it's confirmed.
- Testimonials from "Martha Punla, VP Marketing, Meta" and "Leah Daniel, Design Ops Lead, Teamwork" in the Details.md — these are either placeholder/generated testimonials or they need signed approval and verification before being used publicly. A fabricated testimonial from a Meta VP is a serious reputation risk.
- "Google Gemini (default)" is incorrect per the codebase — OpenRouter is the actual default AI model backend. Marketing copy that says the wrong thing about the technical architecture will be caught by any technical buyer.

**What is genuinely marketable (confirmed from code):**
- **"The only workspace where a task can't start without a definition of done"** — this is a true, unique claim. The Clarity Engine's objective + success criteria fields are database-level reality, not UI decoration.
- **"An AI assistant that reads your actual tasks, docs, and calendar — not just a chatbot"** — the 4-agent specialist architecture with real tool access is real and confirmed in code. This is differentiated.
- **"Meeting transcription with speaker identification built in"** — confirmed. Both Sarvam AI and ElevenLabs providers are implemented with diarization.
- **"One workspace, one price, replaces five tools"** — the pricing math using published competitor list prices is defensible as an illustrative comparison.
- **Sarvam AI's 23-language support** — confirmed in code. For Indian market messaging, this is a genuinely unique selling point vs. English-first competitors.

**Champion trigger:** The CMO can build a clean, honest campaign around the two genuinely unique things: the Clarity Engine philosophy and the context-aware multi-agent AI. Both of these are real and confirmed. The consolidation story is table-stakes but still useful as a secondary message.

**Blocker trigger:**
- Any marketing claim that can't be backed up with code, data, or a real user testimonial is a liability, not an asset. The CMO should clean up the testimonials and user-count claims before scaling any spend.
- "Our brand identity needs to stand for honesty — if we say 7,000 teams and a reporter asks us to prove it, what happens?"

**Their ROI:** A differentiated, defensible one-liner that holds up to scrutiny. Candidates:
1. *"The only task tool that won't let your team start a vague task."* — true, unique, specific.
2. *"An AI assistant that reads your entire workspace — not just a chat window."* — true, differentiated.
3. *"Replace five tools with one. Your AI assistant reads all of it."* — true, combines consolidation + AI angle.

**Fastest objection (internal):** "We can't build a brand on 7,000 teams when we can't prove that number. Let's lead with what we can prove: the product, the integrations, the AI architecture."
